# 1. EU INSTITUTIONS ‚Äî Who Makes & Enforces the Rules?

## **1.1 European Commission**
- Proposes new EU laws (Regulations, Directives, Decisions).  
- Ensures Member States follow EU law (enforcement).  
- Can fine companies for anti-competitive practices.  
- Oversees GDPR application alongside national regulators.  
- Key role in AI Act, DSA, DMA, and Digital Market reforms.

## **1.2 European Parliament**
- Represents EU citizens directly.  
- Votes on laws jointly with the Council.  
- Influences data protection, environmental, competition, and digital policy.

## **1.3 Council of the European Union (‚ÄúCouncil of Ministers‚Äù)**
- Represents the governments of EU Member States.  
- Approves or rejects legislation with Parliament.  
- Sets political priorities.

## **1.4 Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU)**
- Interprets EU law.  
- Ensures uniform application across 27 Member States.  
- Important for landmark GDPR rulings (Schrems I, Schrems II ‚Üí SCC changes).

## **1.5 European Data Protection Board (EDPB)**
- Coordinates all EU Data Protection Authorities.  
- Publishes guidelines, FAQs, and binding decisions.  
- Shapes GDPR interpretation across Europe.

## **1.6 ENISA ‚Äì EU Agency for Cybersecurity**
- Leads cybersecurity standards & recommendations.  
- Works closely on NIS2 implementation.  
- Defines security best practices for cloud, networks, critical infrastructure.

# 2. REGULATIONS vs DIRECTIVES ‚Äî How EU Law Works

## **2.1 EU Regulations**
- Apply **directly** in all EU Member States.  
- No national law required; binding immediately.  
- Ideal for harmonizing digital/tech laws.

### Examples:
- **GDPR** (Data Protection)  
- **DSA** (Digital Services Act)  
- **DMA** (Digital Markets Act)  
- **AI Act**

 Companies operating in EU must follow them instantly.

---

## **2.2 EU Directives**
- Set **goals**, but each Member State decides *how* to achieve them.  
- Must be transposed into national law.  
- Creates slight cross-country differences.

### Examples:
- **NIS2** (Cybersecurity)  
- **Whistleblower Directive**  
- **ePrivacy Directive**

 Compliance requires checking *both* EU Directive + local implementation.

## **3.1 GDPR ‚Äî General Data Protection Regulation**
- Core EU privacy law.  
- Governs personal data collection, usage, storage, transfers.  
- Applies to **EU data subjects worldwide**.  
- Heavy fines (up to ‚Ç¨20M or 4% of global turnover).  
- Key concepts: lawful basis, DPIA, ROPA, DSR, SCCs, retention, security.

**Why it matters**: Every IT, SaaS, AI, FinTech, HealthTech company must implement GDPR.

---

  - Telecom  
  - Finance  
  - Health tech  
  - IoT  
  - Energy  
- Requires:
  - Security controls  
  - Incident reporting  
  - Supply chain risk management  
  - Governance  
  - Cyber hygiene training  

**Why it matters**: Any tech company in Europe will demand NIS2 readiness.

- Takedown procedures

- Risk assessments  

**Why it matters**: Any platform or marketplace operating in the EU is bound by DSA.

#  Summary 

- EU laws are built by Commission + Parliament + Council.  
- Enforcement comes from national regulators + EDPB + CJEU.  
- Regulations apply directly (GDPR, AI Act, DSA, DMA).
